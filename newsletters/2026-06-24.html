🪄 Next-Edit is live, powered by Inception’s Mercury Edit 2
Next-Edit predicts what should come next based on your recent snippets, file context, and edit history. It runs on a diffusion model from Inception and behaves like a co-editor that anticipates your next logical code change.
For the next month, it is free for everyone in Kilo. No trial to start, no card to enter. Just open Kilo and Next-Edit is on. After July 23, Mercury Edit 2 continues at affordable market rates via the Gateway.

🔌 Augment is sunsetting its IDE extensions. Kilo is the JetBrains home they are leaving behind.
Augment users in PyCharm and IntelliJ started getting one-month sunset notices for the extension this week, with the free Community plan removed entirely and pricing moving to a $100/month Business tier. Augment is refocusing on enterprise and its Cosmos agent platform, which makes individual developers and small teams a different kind of customer than the one they want now.
You don’t have to leave the IDE to get back what you had. Kilo Code runs natively in JetBrains with autocomplete, a chat panel, agent mode, worktrees, and free models to start. Install from the JetBrains Marketplace in a couple of minutes, or follow our setup guide for the early-access v7 build.

📊 Terminal Bench scores now live in your model picker
Generic benchmarks are saturating and per-token pricing hides what a model really costs. Starting this week, the Kilo CLI and VS Code extension show Terminal Bench completion scores and average attempt cost in the same place you already check price and context limits.
Score and cost together give you value-per-dollar in your tool. A model that completes 74% of tasks at $0.52 per attempt is a different choice than one that completes 54% at $1.83, even if the second model looks better somewhere else. The full table lives at kilo.ai/leaderboard and updates without a new release.
